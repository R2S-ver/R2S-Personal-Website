PRJ_01 // TEXTILE_ENGINEERING

Modular Ballistic Carrier

I wanted to understand how heavy-duty tactical gear is actually made, so I built my own modular vest from scratch. It wasn't just about making it look cool—I taught myself industrial sewing, pattern drafting, and how to work with tough composite materials to make something genuinely functional.

Process Focus:
1. Patterning: Sketching and cutting flat patterns that fit the body right.
2. Materials: Hunting down the right abrasion-resistant nylons and hardware.
3. Construction: Learning how to stitch through thick layers of Kevlar and foam.
4. Modularity: Setting up a proper MOLLE grid system so pouches can snap right on.

MATERIAL_SPECIFICATIONS

Outer Carrier1000D Nylon Oxford (0.5-1mm)
Soft ArmorLayered Kevlar (0.3-0.5mm/layer)
Hard ArmorArmor Steel Plate (8mm)
Trauma PadPU Foam (10mm)
Inner Carrier1000D Nylon Oxford (0.5-1mm)
Function LayerHeat Dissipation Mesh (15mm)
